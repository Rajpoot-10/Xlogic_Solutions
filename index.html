What is a QR Code?

Ever noticed a square-shaped code on a product, flyer, or website and wondered what it does? That's a QR code — short for "Quick Response." Originally developed in Japan in the 1990s, QR codes have become a widely used tool for instantly accessing information, especially on mobile devices. These two-dimensional barcodes can store data such as text, URLs, and even images.

Scanning a QR code is easy — just use a smartphone or tablet with a camera. Most modern devices can scan QR codes directly using the built-in camera app. Simply point it at the code, and the content will appear instantly. If your device doesn't support this feature by default, a free QR code reader app can do the job.

How QR Codes Can Support Your Business

QR codes can be used for many purposes such as:

  • Marketing and advertising: They can be used on business cards, flyers, or posters to provide more information about a product or service.
  • Online shopping: QR codes can be used on product packaging or on store screens to provide more information about a product or to allow customers to make purchases.
  • Swipe payments: Certain codes can be used to make swipe payments, allowing customers to pay by QR code for products or services without cash or a credit card.

Overall, QR codes are a simple but effective means of sharing information and communicating with customers. Whether you're a business or a consumer, understanding them will help you get the most out of this innovative technology.

Important Things to Know Before Creating a QR Code

Here are some important things to consider when generating a QR code for your business:

Your business goals

Before creating a QR code, it's essential to be clear about your goal. Are you aiming to drive traffic to your website, share detailed product information, or promote a special discount? Defining the purpose of your QR code ensures it aligns with your marketing objectives and delivers value to your audience.

Using distinct QR codes across different campaigns also allows you to track scan activity and user engagement. This data can provide valuable insights, helping you optimize your strategy and boost your overall return on investment (ROI).

QR code usability

QR codes are a powerful tool in modern marketing, but they must be used properly to ensure a smooth user experience. When generating a QR code, make sure it's large enough to be easily scanned and has strong contrast between the code and its background. A minimum size of 2 cm by 2 cm is recommended for reliable scanning. Additionally, using the correct image format and a high-resolution version helps avoid issues with blurry or unreadable codes.

Larger QR codes can be scanned from greater distances, but they still need to be printed clearly and placed where users can see and scan them easily. Always test the QR code on different devices and from various distances before going live to ensure optimal performance.
